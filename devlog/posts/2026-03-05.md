# Email shenanigans

I need to stop writing about AI for a little bit. So today we are going to talk about my attempt at a new email setup.

I have two drivers for a new email: 1. i have this domain, so it would be nice if my email went through this domain and 2. protonmail is cool and all but its quite expensive and has limitations on what you can do with a custom domain. Also, cause its all encrypted and shit you can't use normal email clients like thunderbird.

So i did some poking around and found Migadu. Seems pretty cool - its essentially a webservice backend. No webmail offerings or anything, but you connect your domain and then you have a mailbox which you can login from email clients via normal imap/smtp. They offer a bunch of subscription levels, but the neat part is they are based on total account usage (e.g. 200 inbound emails, 20 outbound emails a day - for 19USD a year) with no limits on things like number of domains linked or number of mailboxes.

There is one somewhat catch in that they don't currently do encryption at rest - i did go backwards and forwards on this, and ultimately decided yolo - its not like gmail, microsoft and anyone else i _send_ an email to isnt gonna be reading my emails anyway. There is a slight data leak risk if they were to incorrectly dispose of hard drives or something, but this is quite a hypothetical. And I can always archive old emails separately and delete them from their server to minimise this risk further.

So now lets talk about some of the fun things they let you do - when you log into migadu, you basically get a massive admin panel to play with. Some of the things you can do are create new mailboxes, create email aliases and create sub-identities under mailboxes.

Since i had a toplevel domain with my name on it, I've seen this fun idea to create different email addresses for individual usecases. For example hello@ could be a public use one, but then create something like shopping@ for online shopping, etc - you get the idea. Initially I approached this as if I would have to create multiple inboxes, but this looked very annoying. An inbox has its own separate login, and is treated as its own recipient in mail clients, meaning id have to "sign in" to a bunch of different inboxes. Annoying.

I was poking around a bit more, and came across _aliases_ (ok fine a actually just asked claude). Aliases let you have multiple different addresses that forward to a base email address. This is cool, because you can have 1 mailbox, and multiple different addresses pointing at it - meaning you only have to sign in to one place. Email clients can also filter / colour / whatever based on the "to" address of your mail - and if you configure them right, can also set the "from" address and the "reply to" of your reply mail to keep the whole illusion of separate email addresses going, whilst all being managed managed from one inbox.

This lets us get into some really overkill stuff that you really dont need to do but its fun to overcomplicate things (if they wernt already). A hypothetical scenario that you wouldnt really actually do but is "nice to have the option of" is if you started receiving spam on a certain address you would a) roughly know where it was leaked from, given it is tied to a specific set of services and b) in theory could disable that alias and switch your services over to a new alias, sidestepping the spam. To take this one step further, I started thinking "wait, so my base email doesn't even need to be a public email" (up to this point, i was probably going to use the "hello" or something as the base email alias). Again, another ridiculous hypothetical but the only address that is hard to "get rid of" is the base mailbox one - that requires moving all the saved emails, updating your email clients etc etc. So to top off this whole house of cards, we make our "base" email address a random string of letters / numbers that only we know (and dont share for use) and only use for logging into our email services and administering stuff.

At this point, we are now treating our base email as a "sensitive" address - which leads us back to considering what happens when we "reply" from an alias. Our mail client may set the "from" field and the "reply-to" fields to the correct alias, but we are still authenticated to the server as our base address - there is a risk that somewhere in the email headers, our "base" email leaks out - oh no!

Never fear! This is where identities come into play. To keep it short (because clearly ive been doing that this entire time) identities let you "be" different authenticated users, all from the one inbox / mail account. This means you can set up your email client to send as someone, and all your headers and whatever other email nonsense will be configured to correctly point at that identity address. You can have as many identities as you like per mailbox and identities also act as aliases on receive, so they are exactly what we've been looking for.

For my final act - I would also like to add something cool I discovered whilst looking at all of this - migadu has an API. This opens things to up be _very_ interesting, like for example having a lil app which can act as a personal "hide my email" service - we can hit the api, set up a new email identity, and bing bang boom thats literally what those services do for you under the hood.

Now if you are like me, following along flying high on your techno-nerd rush of having a service that lets you configure every fuckin option under the sun cause "goddamit i like to fiddle with things" - i unfortunately have to rain on our parade here and bring us crashing down to earth with a reality check.

I set all this up this morning, paid my 19USD for the base tier of usage - all good. Got into work and decided to set up thunderbird with my fancy new email, along with my work email on my laptop - all good. Signed in to everything, sent a test email between my new email and my work outlook - slightly slow, but all good (shockingly, i was expecting my email to get send to spam but it didnt). Anyway, get back from lunch this afternoon, check my email - not all good, cant log in. Wtf. Try again, no good. I even created a new test mailbox to see if i had messed up some configuration - nup. Check the service status page - all green. _Then_ i figured i should check the _real_ status page - r/migadu - and hey presto what do you know, someones posted two hours ago that they cant log in, and everyone else is like "yea me too". We're on like a ~3 hour and counting outage, on my first day of using the service 🥲.

So yea, anyway, ive decided i might just pay for protonmail...
